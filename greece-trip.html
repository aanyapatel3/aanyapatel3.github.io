🧭At a Glance

Relaxed & romantic · beaches, swimming & boat days · no rental car needed anywhere.

✈️ Arrive Athens · Jul 6 🏛️ Athens · 2 nights 🏖️ Naxos · 2 nights 🚤 Koufonisia · boat day ⛵ Paros · 2 nights ✈️ Fly home · Jul 12
Getting around: Athens = metro + walking · Naxos = local bus + boat · Koufonisia = day cruise · Paros = walk Naoussa + boat. Zero driving.

📅Day by Day

Day 1 · Sun Jul 6

Arrive Athens

  • Metro/taxi from airport to Plaka, check in & settle.
  • Golden-hour stroll through Plaka & Anafiotika.
  • 🍽️ Easy rooftop dinner with lit-up Acropolis views.
Day 2 · Mon Jul 7

Athens Highlights

  • Acropolis at 8am (beat heat & crowds) + Acropolis Museum.
  • Lunch in Plaka, browse Monastiraki flea market.
  • Sunset from Lycabettus or Filopappou Hill.
  • 🌅 Optional: Cape Sounion sunset (Temple of Poseidon).
Day 3 · Tue Jul 8

Ferry to Naxos

  • Morning fast ferry Athens → Naxos (~3h15).
  • Check in; afternoon swim at Agios Prokopios / Agia Anna (local bus).
  • Sunset at the Portara (marble temple gate).
Day 4 · Wed Jul 9

🚤 Koufonisia Boat Day

  • Full-day cruise from Naxos to Koufonisia & the Small Cyclades.
  • Swim stops in turquoise coves, Pori beach, Kato Koufonisi.
  • Sunset cruise back to Naxos.
Day 5 · Thu Jul 10

Ferry to Paros

  • Short ferry Naxos → Paros (25–50 min).
  • Base in Naoussa; afternoon swim at Kolymbithres.
  • Evening: wander the old harbor lanes.
Day 6 · Fri Jul 11

Paros Beach + Boat

  • ⛵ Morning boat trip to Antiparos & Despotiko (or beach day at Santa Maria).
  • Last romantic sunset dinner in Naoussa.
Day 7 · Sat Jul 12

Return & Fly Home

  • Morning fast ferry Paros → Piraeus (~2h40), then airport.
  • ⚠️ Safe only if your flight is after ~2:30pm. If earlier, sleep near the airport the night before.

🍽️The Dining Plan

Built for foodies — what to eat each night, where, and what to order. Restaurant picks from the Find Us Lost Greece guides.

🏛️ Athens

Day 1 · Dinner
Rooftop with Acropolis view — Couleur Locale or 360°
Order: a few mezze, grilled octopus, Greek salad, a glass of Assyrtiko. Keep it light after the flight.
Day 2 · Lunch
Saita ★ (Plaka)
Order: their signature bakaliaros skordalia (battered salt cod + fierce garlic dip) — a rare authentic Plaka taverna.
Day 2 · Dinner
Akra ★
Order: let the rotating organic daily menu lead. After, wine at Materia Prima (pick your own bottle) or dessert at Kokkion (artisanal gelato).
What to eat in Athens: a proper souvlaki/gyro for a cheap lunch, loukoumades (honey doughnuts) for dessert, and Greek coffee. Alt dinner: Taverna ton Filon (classic Greek, modern spin).

🏖️ Naxos

Day 3 · Dinner (arrival)
Kastro Naxian Cuisine ★
Order: saganaki made with melted Naxian gruyère, on the terrace below the Kastro walls overlooking the harbor.
Day 4 · Lunch
On the boat / Kalantos marina
Order: whatever's fresh and grilled at the cruise lunch stop — likely fish + salad with a sea view.
Day 4 · Dinner
To Elliniko (Old Town) — or taxi to Axiotissa ★
Order: local Naxian cheeses, slow-cooked lamb, anything with Naxian potatoes. Axiotissa (farm-to-table, FUL favorite) is worth a taxi if you want the standout meal.
What to eat in Naxos: famous Naxian potatoes, graviera & arseniko cheeses, and kitron (local citron liqueur) as a digestif. Also great: Dal Professore (fresh pasta, harbor sunset).

⛵ Paros (Naoussa)

Day 5 · Dinner (arrival)
Siparos ★
Order: imaginative seafood pasta with local Parian ingredients. (Quiet-alley alt: Soso, Greek fusion.)
Day 6 · Lunch
Beach / harbor taverna after the boat trip
Order: gouna (sun-dried mackerel) — a Parian specialty — with a cold beer.
Day 6 · Dinner (last night)
Sigi Ikthios ★ (old harbor square)
Order: fresh fish by the kilo + ouzo, watching the boats. Institutions nearby: Barbarossa (since the '60s) & Minoa (since 1977).
What to eat in Paros: revithada (slow-baked chickpea stew), fresh-caught fish, local Parian wine, and karpouzopita (watermelon pie) for dessert.

📞 Reserve ahead (peak July)

  • Naxos Day 4 — Axiotissa (if going) — books out
  • Paros Day 5 — Siparos dinner

💡Quick Tips

  • Book now: July is peak — ferries, the Naoussa hotels, and the Koufonisia cruise sell out.
  • Water shoes for rocky swim coves; reef-safe sunscreen.
  • Cash for tavernas, beach bars & small boat operators.
  • Naxos buses run often along the beach strip; grab a taxi for remote tavernas.
  • Build buffer on travel days — ferries can run 15–30 min late.
  • Athens airport metro to the center is ~€9 and easy.
