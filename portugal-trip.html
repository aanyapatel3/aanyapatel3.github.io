🧭At a Glance

Three bases, no backtracking: city time in Lisbon (with Sintra), a wine-country stay at Six Senses Douro Valley, then Porto — flying home from Porto.

✈️ Arrive Lisbon (LIS) 🏛️ Lisbon · 3 nights 🏰 Sintra · day trip 🍷 Six Senses Douro Valley · 3 nights 🏙️ Porto · 2 nights ✈️ Fly home · Porto (OPO)
Why this order: open in Lisbon (easy arrival + the Sintra day trip you love), head north to the Douro Valley for three nights at Six Senses, then finish in Porto and fly out of Porto (OPO). You unpack just three times. Train Lisbon→Porto, a short transfer to the Douro; Uber/Bolt within the cities.

📅Day by Day

Day 1

Arrive → Lisbon

  • Midday Land at Lisbon (LIS) — the airport sits right in the city. Uber/Bolt to center ~15–20 min, ~€15
  • Afternoon Check in, then wander Chiado & Príncipe Real — boutiques & miradouros. 🍽️ First bites: a bifana at O Trevo on Praça Luís de Camões (Bourdain's favorite) and a Manteigaria pastel de nata. 📍
  • 6:30pm Sunset at Miradouro de São Pedro de Alcântara with a glass of white port. 📍
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: Belcanto (José Avillez, 2★) for a celebratory opener — or the buzzier 100 Maneiras.
  • 10:30pm 🍸 Nightcap in Bairro Alto/Cais do Sodré — Boavista Social Club, Baz or Vago. 📍
Keep night one easy. If your flight lands late, skip Belcanto for arrival night (it books weeks out anyway) and do a relaxed dinner at A Cevicheria or Prado instead — save the tasting menu for Day 2.
Day 2

Lisbon — Alfama & Belém

  • 9:00am Morning in Alfama — the old Moorish quarter. Ride Tram 28 a few stops, climb to Castelo de São Jorge for the city-and-river view.
  • 12:00pm 🍽️ Lunch: grilled chicken at A Valenciana — or grab piri-piri takeaway from Frangasqueira Nacional to picnic by the river. 📍
  • 1:00pm Taxi to Belém. See the Jerónimos Monastery & Belém Tower (UNESCO). ~15 min
  • 2:00pm 🍽️ The pilgrimage: warm Pastéis de Belém from the original 1837 bakery — and grab a bake at Maruoko (cult Japanese bakery nearby). 📍
  • 3:00pm MAAT riverfront museum + a walk along the Tagus.
  • 4:30pm LX Factory — the converted industrial complex under the bridge: design shops, street art, cafés. ~5 min from Belém 📍
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: Belcanto (if not Day 1) or Alma (2★) — or go casual at Karater (Georgian) or Taberna Carolina (Chiado).
Day 3

🏰 Sintra Day Trip

  • 8:30am To Sintra — a fairy-tale of palaces in the hills. Uber/Bolt ~40 min (~€35–45), train from Rossio ~40 min, or a private driver 📍
  • 9:30am Pena Palace early (beat the crowds) — the candy-colored romantic palace on the peak.
  • 11:30am Quinta da Regaleira — gardens, grottoes & the spiral Initiation Well.
  • 1:30pm 🍽️ Lunch: Incomum or Tascantiga in Sintra village.
  • 3:00pm On the way back: Cabo da Roca (mainland Europe's westernmost point), wild Praia do Guincho beach, then Cascais seaside. 📍
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner back in Lisbon: Cervejaria Ramiro — garlic shrimp & seafood, finished with a prego — or Tasca Bica for the 6-day dry-aged chicken.
  • Late Still hungry? Galeto, the iconic 24-hr diner, for a late bite. 📍
🚗 Need a private driver? No — it's optional. Uber & Bolt work all over Lisbon and out to Sintra. The only catch is up at the palaces: parking is chaos and Ubers get scarce at peak, so people use the local 434 bus, tuk-tuks, or pre-book one driver to wait. Pre-book Pena Palace timed tickets either way.
Day 4

🍷 Lisbon → Douro Valley · Settle In

  • 9:00am Alfa Pendular train Lisboa → Porto (Comfort/1st class). ~2h50 — or a private door-to-door transfer the whole way.
  • 12:30pm Scenic transfer Porto → the Douro Valley, the world's oldest demarcated wine region. ~1h30, vineyards the whole way
  • 2:30pm Check in at Six Senses Douro Valley (a restored 19th-c. manor above Régua). 🍽️ Late lunch on the terrace with a glass of white port.
  • 4:30pm Ease in — estate vineyard stroll, then a guided tasting in the Wine Library (a deep Portuguese list to learn the region by).
  • 6:30pm Sunset over the valley; first dip in the vineyard infinity pool.
  • 8:00pm 🍽️ Dinner: Vale de Abraão at the resort, relaxed after the travel day.
🚆 Travel day — keep it light. Take the Alfa Pendular to Porto (book early on CP.pt) and have Six Senses arrange the transfer from the station — you'll be at the resort by mid-afternoon with the evening free to settle in. Save the touring for tomorrow.
Day 5

🍇 The Big Wine Day — Pinhão & the Quintas

  • 9:00am Breakfast over the vines, then to Peso da Régua station. ~10 min from Six Senses
  • 10:00am 🚂 Scenic train Régua → Pinhão — one of the world's prettiest rail rides, hugging the river past the terraces (open windows in summer). ~25 min · a private driver meets you in Pinhão
  • 11:00am Quinta tour & tasting — Quinta do Bomfim (Dow's / Symington) or Quinta Nova ("9 must-see wineries in the world"): cellars, vineyards, a flight of ports & Douro reds.
  • 1:00pm 🍽️ Vineyard lunch with a view & wine pairing — Quinta de la Rosa or Quinta do Bomfim in Pinhão (both from your Douro guide).
  • 3:30pm ⛵ 1-hour rabelo boat tour from Pinhão — the traditional flat-bottomed boats, gliding through the terraced bends (the prettiest way to see the valley).
  • 5:00pm Stop at the Casal de Loivos viewpoint over the Pinhão bend on the way back. 📍
  • 8:00pm 🍽️ Dinner at Six Senses with sommelier pairings (ask about the heated-tong opening of a vintage Port).
🚂 Make the train the centerpiece: the Régua → Pinhão stretch of the Douro line is a bucket-list ride (tickets are cheap; book the river side). Have your private driver-guide meet you in Pinhão for the quintas, boat & viewpoint — Six Senses can arrange the whole day.
Day 6

🌿 Slow Douro — Active Morning, Spa Afternoon

  • 9:00am Pick your morning — a low-difficulty vineyard walk (Provesende → Pinhão), a Six Senses experience (wine blending, organic-garden tour, or sunrise yoga), or the scenic drive to the famous São Leonardo de Galafura viewpoint over the river. ~2h 📍
  • 11:00am Or a half-day in Lamego — climb the 686 steps to the Santuário de Nossa Senhora dos Remédios, then a tasting at Quinta do Tedo on the way back. ~15–20 min from Régua
  • 1:00pm 🍽️ Light lunch — Jardim da Zita (Lamego) or back at the resort.
  • 3:00pm One signature tasting — Quinta de Nápoles (Niepoort) or a guided vertical port tasting in the resort's Wine Library.
  • 4:30pm Spa + infinity pool — the afternoon to do nothing at all.
  • 8:00pm 🍽️ Last Douro dinner: DOC by Rui Paula — glass dining room right on the river at Folgosa (book ahead). ~20 min 📋
🌿 Three nights means you don't have to cram. Day 5 is the deep-dive; today is balance — pick one thing (viewpoints, Lamego, a tasting, or a walk), then genuinely switch off. Six Senses arranges the walk, tasting, spa or a driver.

Day 7

Douro → Porto

  • 10:30am Slow morning at the resort, then transfer to Porto. ~1h30
  • 1:00pm Check in, then 🍽️ lunch in the Ribeira — Taberna dos Mercadores or DAMA pé de cabra (both your Beli), by the riverfront & the iconic Dom Luís I Bridge. 📋
  • 4:00pm Livraria Lello (the storybook bookshop) & São Bento station's blue azulejo hall.
  • 6:00pm Cross to Vila Nova de Gaia for a port cellar tour & tasting (Graham's, Taylor's or Sandeman) at golden hour.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: Cozinha das Flores or Euskalduna Studio (your Beli) — or Antiqvvm above the river.
  • 10:30pm 🍷 Natural-wine nightcap at Genuíno (your Beli). 📍
Day 8

Porto at Leisure

  • 9:30am Slow brunch (Zenith or do Norte), then the Bolsa Palace & Clérigos Tower climb.
  • 12:30pm 🍽️ Lunch: the famous francesinha at Café Santiago — or one of your Beli spots: Gruta (Brazilian), MISTU (Mediterranean) or Pata Gorda.
  • 2:30pm Tram to Foz do Douro — where the river meets the Atlantic; oceanfront promenade & a coffee.
  • 5:00pm Last stroll through Cedofeita design shops & galleries (peek at Beli spots Apego & Atrevo nearby).
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Last dinner: The Yeatman (2★) or Pedro Lemos (1★) in Foz — or Le Monument (all your Beli).
Day 9

Fly Home ✈️ — from Porto

  • Morning Last pastel de nata, then to Porto Airport (OPO). ~25–30 min from the center by Uber/Bolt or metro
  • Midday ✈️ Fly home from Porto. 🎉
🛫 OPO is compact and easy. For long-haul you'll likely connect through Lisbon or a European hub — leave a comfortable buffer.

💡Quick Tips

  • Book the marquee tables & Six Senses early: Belcanto, Alma, The Yeatman and Six Senses all fill weeks ahead.
  • Open-jaw flight (in Lisbon, out Porto) saves a backtrack.
  • Lisbon & Porto are hilly — comfortable shoes for the cobblestones.
  • Pre-book Sintra's Pena Palace timed entry and go right at opening.
  • Uber & Bolt are cheap and everywhere in the cities — no rental car needed; let Six Senses handle the Douro transfers.
  • Tipping: ~5–10% at restaurants is generous and appreciated.
  • Shoulder season (May/Jun, Sep/Oct) is ideal — warm, fewer crowds, the Douro is gorgeous.
