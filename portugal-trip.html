🧭At a Glance

Elevated & relaxed · city design hotels, a Douro wine estate, and a relaxed Algarve coast finish — north to south, no backtracking.

✈️ Arrive Lisbon (LIS) 🏛️ Lisbon · 3 nights 🏰 Sintra · day trip 🚄 Porto · 3 nights 🍷 Douro Valley · wine day 🏖️ Algarve · 3 nights ⛵ Benagil caves ✈️ Fly home · Faro (FAO)
Why this order: open in Lisbon (easy long-haul arrival), train north to Porto for the Douro, then south to the Algarve to wind down on the coast and fly out of Faro. You unpack just three times. Trains city-to-city; Uber/Bolt within the cities; a rental car only for the Algarve coast.
🍷🏖️⛵ Built around what you love: wine tours (a full Douro day with quinta tastings + a private vintage-boat cruise), beaches (the Algarve's golden cliffs & calm coves), and boat days & swimming (Benagil sea caves, a sunset cruise, snorkel stops). Every leg has at least one of the three.

📅Day by Day

Day 1

Arrive → Lisbon

  • Midday Land at Lisbon (LIS) — the airport sits right in the city. Taxi/Uber to center ~15–20 min, ~€15
  • Afternoon Check in, then ease in with a wander through Chiado & Príncipe Real — boutiques, miradouros (viewpoints), a first pastel de nata.
  • 6:30pm Sunset at Miradouro de São Pedro de Alcântara with a glass of white port. 📍
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: Belcanto (José Avillez, 2★) for a celebratory opener — or the buzzier 100 Maneiras.
Keep night one easy. If your flight lands late, skip Belcanto for arrival night (it books weeks out anyway) and do a relaxed dinner at A Cevicheria or Prado instead — save the tasting menu for Day 2.
Day 2

Lisbon — Alfama & Belém

  • 9:00am Morning in Alfama — the old Moorish quarter. Ride Tram 28 a few stops, climb to Castelo de São Jorge for the city-and-river view.
  • 12:30pm Taxi to Belém. See the Jerónimos Monastery & Belém Tower (UNESCO). ~15 min from center
  • 1:30pm 🍽️ The pilgrimage: warm Pastéis de Belém straight from the original 1837 bakery.
  • 3:00pm MAAT riverfront museum + a walk along the Tagus.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: Belcanto (if not Day 1) or Alma (2★) — Lisbon's two great tasting tables.
Day 3

🏰 Sintra Day Trip

  • 8:30am To Sintra — a fairy-tale of palaces in the hills. Uber/Bolt ~40 min (~€35–45), train from Rossio ~40 min, or a private driver 📍
  • 9:30am Pena Palace early (beat the crowds) — the candy-colored romantic palace on the peak.
  • 11:30am Quinta da Regaleira — gardens, grottoes & the spiral Initiation Well.
  • 1:30pm 🍽️ Lunch: Incomum or Tascantiga in Sintra village.
  • 3:00pm Optional Cabo da Roca (mainland Europe's westernmost point) + Cascais seaside on the way back.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner back in Lisbon: Cervejaria Ramiro — legendary garlic shrimp & seafood, finished with a prego steak roll.
🚗 Need a private driver? No — it's optional. Uber & Bolt work all over Lisbon and out to Sintra. The only catch is up at the palaces: parking is chaos and Ubers get scarce at peak, so people use the local 434 bus, tuk-tuks, or just pre-book one driver to wait and hop you between Pena, Regaleira, Cabo da Roca & Cascais — convenient, not required. Pre-book Pena Palace timed tickets either way.
Day 4

🚄 Train to Porto

  • 10:00am Alfa Pendular train Lisboa → Porto (Comfort/1st class). ~2h50, station Campanhã/São Bento
  • 1:30pm Check in, lunch, then explore the Ribeira riverfront & the iconic Dom Luís I Bridge.
  • 4:00pm Livraria Lello (the storybook bookshop) & São Bento station's blue azulejo hall.
  • 6:30pm Cross to Vila Nova de Gaia for a port cellar tour & tasting (Graham's, Taylor's or Sandeman) at golden hour.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: Cantinho do Avillez or Euskalduna Studio.
🚆 Book the Alfa Pendular early on CP.pt for the best fares and Conforto (1st-class) seats. São Bento is right in the center; Campanhã is the main hub — both work.
Day 5

🍷 Douro Valley Wine Day

  • 8:30am Private chauffeured wine tour into the Douro Valley — the world's oldest demarcated wine region, terraced vineyards plunging to the river. ~1h30 drive each way
  • 10:30am Private guided visit & tasting at a historic quinta — Quinta de Nápoles (Niepoort) or Quinta Nova (one of the "9 must-see wineries in the world").
  • 12:30pm ⛵ Private vintage-boat cruise on the Douro — a classic 1957 wooden yacht gliding through the terraces (the boat from your private-tour quote). Swim/relax stops as you go.
  • 1:30pm 🍽️ Vineyard lunch with valley views — Quinta Nova's Terraçu's or DOC by Rui Paula on the river, with wine pairing.
  • 4:00pm Second estate & tasting — Quinta do Crasto or Quinta do Bomfim (Dow's), terrace overlooking the bends.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner back in Porto: Antiqvvm (1★) or, for the full splurge, The Yeatman (2★).
🍇 Make it effortless: a private Douro tour (~€400–600 for two, lunch & tastings + cruise) beats the group vans — exactly the kind of day in your Luxury Tours Portugal quote. Stay over to go deeper: a 1-night add-on at Six Senses Douro Valley (vineyard spa + garden BBQ) turns this into a proper wine escape — highly recommended given how much you love wine days.
Day 6

Porto at Leisure

  • 9:30am Slow brunch (Zenith or do Norte), then the Bolsa Palace & Clérigos Tower climb.
  • 12:30pm 🍽️ Lunch: the famous francesinha at Café Santiago — or a perfect bifana at Conga.
  • 2:30pm Tram to Foz do Douro — where the river meets the Atlantic; oceanfront promenade & a coffee.
  • 5:00pm Last stroll through Cedofeita design shops & galleries.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: Pedro Lemos (1★) in Foz, or casual seafood at Marisqueira Antiga.
Day 7

🏖️ Travel to the Algarve

  • Morning Fly Porto → Faro (~1h, fastest) — or train via Lisbon (scenic but ~6–7h). Pick up a rental car at Faro (FAO)
  • Afternoon Drive to your coast base (Lagos / Carvoeiro / Vilamoura). Check in, settle into the resort.
  • 5:00pm First beach: Praia Dona Ana or Praia da Marinha — golden cliffs & turquoise water.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: resort restaurant or grilled fish on the marina.
✈️ Porto→Faro by air saves a full day vs. the train. A rental car is worth it in the Algarve — beaches and the best restaurants are spread along the coast and not walkable.
Day 8

⛵ Benagil Caves & Beaches

  • 9:30am Boat tour to the Benagil Sea Cave — the famous domed cave with the sky-hole, plus hidden grottoes along the cliffs.
  • 12:00pm Swim & sunbathe at Praia da Marinha (one of Europe's most beautiful beaches).
  • 1:30pm 🍽️ Lunch: cliffside at O Bicho or fresh seafood in Carvoeiro.
  • 4:00pm Spa & pool afternoon at the resort — you've earned it.
  • 8:00pm 🍽️ Dinner: Vila Joya (2★) or Ocean at Vila Vita (2★) — the Algarve's destination tables.
⛵ Do Benagil right: a small-group or private boat (~€35–60 pp group / ~€250 private) at 9–10am beats the midday crowds and lets you actually go inside the cave. Kayak/SUP tours land on the cave beach itself.
Day 9

Lagos & the Cliffs

  • 9:30am Ponta da Piedade — dramatic ochre rock arches & sea stacks; walk the cliff boardwalk above Lagos.
  • 12:00pm Old-town Lagos — cobbled streets, the marina, a long lunch.
  • 3:00pm Beach pick: Praia do Camilo (Lagos) or chic beach club at Quinta do Lago.
  • 6:30pm Sunset drinks at a cliff bar.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Last dinner: Bon Bon (1★) or a relaxed seafood feast at A Casa do Avô.
Day 10

Fly Home ✈️ — from Faro

  • Morning Slow breakfast, last beach walk, return the rental car at Faro (FAO).
  • Midday ✈️ Fly home from Faro — or connect via Lisbon if needed. Faro is ~40–60 min from most Algarve bases
🛫 Faro has direct flights to many European hubs; for long-haul you'll likely connect through Lisbon. Leave a comfortable buffer to drop the car. 🎉

💡Quick Tips

  • Book the marquee tables early: Belcanto, Alma, The Yeatman, Vila Joya book out weeks ahead — lock them before you fly.
  • Open-jaw flight (in Lisbon, out Faro) saves a day and a backtrack.
  • Lisbon & Porto are hilly — pack comfortable shoes for the cobblestones; the heels can wait for dinner.
  • Pre-book Sintra's Pena Palace timed entry and go right at opening.
  • Benagil at 9–10am beats the crowds and the midday boat traffic.
  • Uber & Bolt are cheap and everywhere in Lisbon & Porto — no need for private cars in the cities (Bolt is often the cheapest).
  • Rent a car only in the Algarve — Uber/Bolt exist there but are sparser, and the best beaches are spread out. You don't want a car in the cities.
  • Tipping: ~5–10% at restaurants is generous and appreciated.
  • Shoulder season (May/Jun, Sep/Oct) is ideal — warm coast, fewer crowds, better rates.
