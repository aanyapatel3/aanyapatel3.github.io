🧭At a Glance

Three bases, no backtracking: city time in Lisbon (with Sintra), a wine-country stay at Six Senses Douro Valley, then Porto — flying home from Porto.

✈️ Arrive Lisbon (LIS) 🏛️ Lisbon · 3 nights 🏰 Sintra · day trip 🍷 Six Senses Douro Valley · 3 nights 🏙️ Porto · 2 nights ✈️ Fly home · Porto (OPO)
Why this order: open in Lisbon (easy arrival + the Sintra day trip you love), head north to the Douro Valley for three nights at Six Senses, then finish in Porto and fly out of Porto (OPO). You unpack just three times. Train Lisbon→Porto, a short transfer to the Douro; Uber/Bolt within the cities.

📅Day by Day

Day 1

Arrive → Lisbon

  • Midday Land at Lisbon (LIS) — the airport sits right in the city. Uber/Bolt to center ~15–20 min, ~€15
  • Afternoon Check in, then ease in with a wander through Chiado & Príncipe Real — boutiques, miradouros (viewpoints), a first pastel de nata.
  • 6:30pm Sunset at Miradouro de São Pedro de Alcântara with a glass of white port. 📍
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: Belcanto (José Avillez, 2★) for a celebratory opener — or the buzzier 100 Maneiras.
Keep night one easy. If your flight lands late, skip Belcanto for arrival night (it books weeks out anyway) and do a relaxed dinner at A Cevicheria or Prado instead — save the tasting menu for Day 2.
Day 2

Lisbon — Alfama & Belém

  • 9:00am Morning in Alfama — the old Moorish quarter. Ride Tram 28 a few stops, climb to Castelo de São Jorge for the city-and-river view.
  • 12:30pm Taxi to Belém. See the Jerónimos Monastery & Belém Tower (UNESCO). ~15 min from center
  • 1:30pm 🍽️ The pilgrimage: warm Pastéis de Belém straight from the original 1837 bakery.
  • 3:00pm MAAT riverfront museum + a walk along the Tagus.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: Belcanto (if not Day 1) or Alma (2★) — Lisbon's two great tasting tables.
Day 3

🏰 Sintra Day Trip

  • 8:30am To Sintra — a fairy-tale of palaces in the hills. Uber/Bolt ~40 min (~€35–45), train from Rossio ~40 min, or a private driver 📍
  • 9:30am Pena Palace early (beat the crowds) — the candy-colored romantic palace on the peak.
  • 11:30am Quinta da Regaleira — gardens, grottoes & the spiral Initiation Well.
  • 1:30pm 🍽️ Lunch: Incomum or Tascantiga in Sintra village.
  • 3:00pm Optional Cabo da Roca (mainland Europe's westernmost point) + Cascais seaside on the way back.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner back in Lisbon: Cervejaria Ramiro — legendary garlic shrimp & seafood, finished with a prego steak roll.
🚗 Need a private driver? No — it's optional. Uber & Bolt work all over Lisbon and out to Sintra. The only catch is up at the palaces: parking is chaos and Ubers get scarce at peak, so people use the local 434 bus, tuk-tuks, or pre-book one driver to wait. Pre-book Pena Palace timed tickets either way.
Day 4

🍷 Lisbon → Douro Valley (Six Senses)

  • 9:00am Alfa Pendular train Lisboa → Porto (Comfort/1st class). ~2h50 — or a private door-to-door transfer the whole way.
  • 12:30pm Transfer Porto → the Douro Valley, the world's oldest demarcated wine region. ~1h30, vineyard views the whole way
  • 2:30pm Check in at Six Senses Douro Valley — a restored 19th-c. manor above the river. Settle in: the spa, the vineyard infinity pool, a welcome glass of port.
  • 5:00pm First tasting & vineyard stroll on the estate.
  • 8:00pm 🍽️ Dinner at the resort — Vale de Abraão or the garden BBQ, with Douro wines.
🚆 Smoothest way: take the Alfa Pendular to Porto (book early on CP.pt) and have Six Senses arrange the transfer from there — or do a private car the whole way so you can stop for photos. Either way you're at the resort by mid-afternoon.
Day 5

🍇 Full Douro Wine Day

  • 9:00am Breakfast over the vines, then a private quinta tour & tasting — Quinta de Nápoles (Niepoort) or Quinta Nova (one of the "9 must-see wineries in the world").
  • 12:30pm ⛵ Douro river cruise — a classic wooden boat gliding through the terraces; swim/relax stops as you go.
  • 1:30pm 🍽️ Vineyard lunch with wine pairing — Quinta Nova's Terraçu's or DOC by Rui Paula on the river.
  • 4:00pm Second estate & tasting (Quinta do Crasto or Quinta do Bomfim), or back to Six Senses for the spa.
  • 8:00pm 🍽️ Dinner at Six Senses — let the sommelier lead the pairings.
🍷 A private Douro day (~€400–600 for two, tastings + cruise + lunch) is the relaxed way to do it — exactly the kind of day in your Luxury Tours Portugal quote, and Six Senses can set it all up.
Day 6

🚲 Douro at Your Own Pace

  • 9:30am Active morning in the vines — a low-difficulty guided hike (Provesende → Pinhão) or a private e-bike tour along the old Régua–Pinhão line. ~2h, mostly downhill, big river views
  • 12:00pm Visit Quinta do Crasto — tasting on the terrace by its famous infinity pool over the bends of the river.
  • 1:30pm 🍽️ Lunch: at the quinta, or back toward Pinhão for a riverside table.
  • 3:30pm Wander Pinhão — the little town with the azulejo-tiled railway station — then back to Six Senses.
  • 5:00pm Spa + vineyard infinity pool afternoon (you're on wine time now).
  • 8:00pm 🍽️ Dinner at Six Senses, or the garden BBQ night if it's on.
🌿 Day two in the valley is for slowing down — pick one activity (hike or e-bike or a second quinta), then let the spa and pool do the rest. Six Senses can arrange a private guide for any of it.
Day 7

Douro → Porto

  • 10:30am Slow morning at the resort, then transfer to Porto. ~1h30
  • 1:00pm Check in, lunch, then explore the Ribeira riverfront & the iconic Dom Luís I Bridge.
  • 4:00pm Livraria Lello (the storybook bookshop) & São Bento station's blue azulejo hall.
  • 6:00pm Cross to Vila Nova de Gaia for a port cellar tour & tasting (Graham's, Taylor's or Sandeman) at golden hour.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Dinner: Cozinha das Flores or Euskalduna Studio.
Day 8

Porto at Leisure

  • 9:30am Slow brunch (Zenith or do Norte), then the Bolsa Palace & Clérigos Tower climb.
  • 12:30pm 🍽️ Lunch: the famous francesinha at Café Santiago — or a perfect bifana at Conga.
  • 2:30pm Tram to Foz do Douro — where the river meets the Atlantic; oceanfront promenade & a coffee.
  • 5:00pm Last stroll through Cedofeita design shops & galleries.
  • 8:30pm 🍽️ Last dinner: The Yeatman (2★) or Pedro Lemos (1★) in Foz, or casual at Cantinho do Avillez.
Day 9

Fly Home ✈️ — from Porto

  • Morning Last pastel de nata, then to Porto Airport (OPO). ~25–30 min from the center by Uber/Bolt or metro
  • Midday ✈️ Fly home from Porto. 🎉
🛫 OPO is compact and easy. For long-haul you'll likely connect through Lisbon or a European hub — leave a comfortable buffer.

💡Quick Tips

  • Book the marquee tables & Six Senses early: Belcanto, Alma, The Yeatman and Six Senses all fill weeks ahead.
  • Open-jaw flight (in Lisbon, out Porto) saves a backtrack.
  • Lisbon & Porto are hilly — comfortable shoes for the cobblestones.
  • Pre-book Sintra's Pena Palace timed entry and go right at opening.
  • Uber & Bolt are cheap and everywhere in the cities — no rental car needed; let Six Senses handle the Douro transfers.
  • Tipping: ~5–10% at restaurants is generous and appreciated.
  • Shoulder season (May/Jun, Sep/Oct) is ideal — warm, fewer crowds, the Douro is gorgeous.
