## 1. Executive summary

**Rating: Long / Buy (high conviction, high risk).** Guardant Health is the clearest pure-play on the secular shift of cancer testing from tissue to blood ("liquid biopsy"). It owns three franchises at very different stages: a profitable-at-the-margin **therapy-selection** business (Guardant360), an early-but-fast **molecular residual disease (MRD)** test (Reveal), and the asset that drives the whole thesis — **Shield**, the first and only FDA-approved blood test for primary colorectal-cancer (CRC) screening, now included in both **American Cancer Society (May 2026)** and NCCN guidelines.

The debate isn't growth — revenue is compounding 30%+ — it's (a) whether Shield can convert a multi-hundred-million-test screening TAM before well-capitalized rivals (Exact Sciences/Freenome) catch up, and (b) whether the company reaches cash-flow breakeven before it needs to raise again. I think the risk/reward skews favourable, but this is a story stock priced at ~11x EV/sales with real cash burn — sizing should reflect that.

**The one-paragraph thesis.** Buy GH as the category leader in liquid biopsy at the exact moment its screening franchise transitions from "FDA-approved but unreimbursed-at-scale" to "guideline-included with a locked-in $1,495 Medicare rate." The core oncology business is already near breakeven and funds the screening land-grab; Shield's ACS/NCCN inclusion in May 2026 is the catalyst that de-risks the largest opportunity. The stock is not cheap and the company still burns ~$200m+/yr, so this is a position to size for volatility — but the multiple is reasonable versus the growth and the optionality, and the competitive moat (first-mover, only FDA primary-screen approval, Medicare ADLT pricing) is real.

## 2. How I approached the model

For a pre-profit, TAM-driven diagnostics name the earnings-multiple toolkit is useless. So I:

1. Built a **bottom-up revenue build by product line** (Oncology incl. Guardant360 + Reveal; Screening = Shield; Biopharma & Data) for FY2024A → FY2025A → FY2026E → FY2027E, and checked the sum against management's FY2026 guidance.
2. Built a **Shield CRC screening TAM / penetration scenario** (eligible population x screening rate x ASP x GH share) — bear/base/bull — because Shield is ~80% of the long-term equity value in my view.
3. Valued the whole company on **EV/sales** scenarios (the right tool for unprofitable hyper-growth diagnostics), cross-checked with a **path-to-FCF-breakeven** sanity test.
4. Framed a **Long/Pass/Short** decision with the 2–3 things that would change my mind.

Shared assumptions: I treat GH as roughly **net-cash-neutral** today (~$1.2bn cash & investments at Q1'26 vs. convertible notes due 2027/2033), so EV ≈ market cap, and I use ~131.5m shares, growing modestly with SBC/convert dilution.

## 3. Business overview — what each product actually is

Guardant sequences cell-free DNA (and increasingly multi-omic signals) from a blood draw to find, characterise and monitor cancer. Four revenue lines:

- **Guardant360 (Oncology / therapy selection).** The cash cow. A liquid (and tissue) comprehensive genomic-profiling test used in **advanced cancer** to match patients to targeted therapies. Well-reimbursed, growing volumes ~30–45%/yr, and the engine that funds everything else. ~$684m of FY2025 oncology revenue.
- **Guardant Reveal (MRD / recurrence monitoring).** A **tissue-free** blood test that looks for **molecular residual disease** — tiny amounts of tumour DNA left after surgery/treatment — to catch recurrence early. Big future market, but GH is the #2 here behind Natera's Signatera. Reported within Oncology.
- **Shield (Screening).** The crown jewel. A **blood-based CRC screening test** for average-risk adults 45+. **First and only blood test FDA-approved as a primary CRC screening option** (2024); ADLT status and **$1,495 Medicare rate**; and as of **May 2026, included in ACS and NCCN guidelines.** This is the multi-billion-dollar option. FY2025 screening revenue $79.7m on ~87k tests.
- **Biopharma & Data.** Selling testing services and data/companion-diagnostic work to pharma (e.g., a Merck collaboration on the Guardant Infinity platform). Steady ~17–18% grower; ~$210m in FY2025.

## 6. Shield CRC screening TAM / penetration scenario (the core value driver)

This is the engine of the thesis, so I model it explicitly rather than waving at "huge TAM."

**The setup.** ~50m+ Americans are unscreened for CRC; the average-risk screening-eligible population (age 45+) is on the order of **~110–120m people**, screened roughly every ~3 years for a blood test. Shield's edge is **adherence**: >90% of patients complete a blood draw vs. far lower completion for colonoscopy/stool tests, and it is the **only FDA-approved blood primary-screen** with a locked **$1,495 Medicare rate (2026–2027)** and now **ACS + NCCN guideline inclusion (May 2026)**. The open prize is **USPSTF inclusion** (currently does not list blood-based testing because none was FDA-approved at the 2021 update), which would unlock no-cost-share coverage and supercharge volumes.

**Scenario framework (steady-state, illustrative ~2030 view):**

| Driver | Bear | **Base** | Bull |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Screening-eligible (45+) addressable, blood-amenable (m) | 60 | **80** | 100 |
| Annual tested-via-blood penetration | 2% | **5%** | 9% |
| Annual Shield tests (m) | 1.2 | **4.0** | 9.0 |
| GH share of blood-based screening | 60% | **70%** | 75% |
| Net Shield tests to GH (m) | 0.72 | **2.8** | 6.75 |
| Blended ASP ($) | 700 | **850** | 1,000 |
| **Implied Shield revenue ($bn)** | **~0.5** | **~2.4** | **~6.8** |

<sub>All figures illustrative ("E"). ASP is blended across Medicare ($1,495 locked) and commercial (ramping with coverage). USPSTF inclusion is the single biggest swing factor between Base and Bull.</sub>

**Why I anchor Base at ~$2.4bn Shield revenue (vs. ~$190m in FY2026E):** even modest single-digit penetration of a ~80m-person pool, every ~3 years, at a sub-$1,000 blended ASP, is a multi-billion line. That is ~2.5x the *entire current company revenue* coming from one product — which is exactly why a high EV/sales multiple on near-term sales can still be defensible: you're paying for a franchise whose steady-state is far larger than today's P&L.

**Honest caveats.** (1) Shield's **sensitivity for advanced adenomas (precancerous polyps) is low (~13%)** — it catches cancer well (~83% sensitivity; 100% for stage II+) but is weaker at prevention than colonoscopy/Cologuard. Guidelines reflect this by positioning Shield for patients who *decline* other screening. (2) Reimbursement ASP could compress as commercial mix grows. (3) Competition (below) will arrive ~2 years behind but is extremely well-funded.

## 7. Competition

| Company / test | Category | Where it stands vs. GH | Threat level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Exact Sciences** — Cologuard / Cologuard Plus (stool) + **Freenome** (blood, licensed) | CRC screening | Dominant incumbent in stool screening (~$3.2bn+ '25 rev); blood test via Freenome **~2 years behind** Shield, likely launch ~late 2026 | **High (long-term)** |
| **Freenome** (now Exact-licensed) | CRC screening (blood) | First-gen ~81% CRC sensitivity, ~14% advanced-lesion — broadly comparable to Shield; not yet FDA-approved | High (via Exact) |
| **Natera** — Signatera | MRD / recurrence | ~$2.3bn '25 revenue, ~$28bn market cap; **virtual monopoly in MRD**; ~800k oncology tests '25 (+52%) | **High (in MRD/Reveal)** |
| **Tempus AI** | Multi-modal onc + MRD | MRD ~6,500 tests Q1'26 (+~500% YoY, small base); data/AI angle | Medium |
| **Grail** — Galleri | Multi-cancer early detection (MCED) | Adjacent (screens many cancers); not FDA-approved; reimbursement unproven | Medium (adjacent) |
| **Roche** (SAGA Diagnostics) | MRD | Recent acquisition; deep pockets, longer-dated | Medium (long-term) |

**Net competitive read.** GH is **clearly ahead in CRC blood screening** (only FDA primary-screen approval, ADLT pricing, guideline inclusion) with a ~2-year lead on Exact/Freenome — the moat that matters most for the thesis. In **MRD**, GH's Reveal is a credible #2 but Natera's Signatera is the runaway leader; I do not underwrite GH winning MRD, so Reveal is upside, not base. The risk is that screening is a *land-grab* — first mover and adherence matter, but a deep-pocketed Exact with an entrenched PCP salesforce could erode share fast once Freenome launches.

## 8. Valuation (bear / base / bull)

GH is unprofitable, so I value it on **EV/forward sales**, applied to FY2027E revenue, cross-checked against a path-to-breakeven. Today GH trades at **~11x EV/revenue** (EV ~$12–13bn / FY2025 $982m), or **~9–10x on FY2026E guide** — a premium to diagnostics peers but in line with hyper-growth liquid-biopsy comps (Natera trades richer on an EV/sales basis given its MRD monopoly).

**Multiple scenarios on my FY2027E revenue (~$1.70bn):**

| Scenario | FY2027E rev | EV/Sales | Implied EV ($bn) | + net cash (~$0) → equity | ÷ ~133m sh | **Price** | vs. ~$127 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Bear** | 1.55 | 7x | 10.9 | 10.9 | | **~$82** | **−35%** |
| **Base** | 1.70 | 10x | 17.0 | 17.0 | | **~$150** | **+18%** |
| **Bull** | 1.85 | 13x | 24.1 | 24.1 | | **~$181** → **~$205** w/ Shield re-rate | **+43% to +61%** |

<sub>Bear assumes Shield disappoints / a competitor launch + multiple compression to ~7x and some convert dilution. Base holds today's ~10x on a slightly higher revenue base — i.e., you make the revenue growth (~30%) less a small de-rate. Bull layers a multiple re-rate (toward Natera-like) as USPSTF/screening visibility improves. Bull upper bound assumes the market starts capitalising the §6 Shield steady-state.</sub>

**Path-to-breakeven cross-check.** Management has guided the **non-screening business to FCF breakeven (targeted around 4Q25)**, with screening the remaining ~$200m/yr investment. Cash & investments ~$1.2bn against ~$200–230m annual burn implies **~4–5 years of runway** even before further oncology operating leverage — so the dilution risk is real but not imminent, and improving (FCF burn −$275m '24 → −$233m '25, narrowing). If Shield scales as in my Base TAM, the screening burn flips to contribution and the whole company turns FCF-positive late-decade. That self-funding inflection is what justifies paying ~10x sales rather than treating burn as terminal.

**My 12-month base target: ~$150 (+18%).** Skewed to the upside given the Shield optionality not yet in the P&L.

## 9. Bull case

1. **Shield is a genuine category creator with a widening moat.** Only FDA-approved blood primary CRC screen, locked $1,495 Medicare rate through 2027, ~2-year lead on Exact/Freenome, >90% patient completion, and — the May 2026 catalyst — **ACS + NCCN guideline inclusion**. The next domino, **USPSTF inclusion**, would unlock no-cost-share coverage and is the bull's path to the §6 Base/Bull TAM.
2. **The core is already self-funding the option.** Oncology (Guardant360) is a 30%+ grower at 66% gross margin near breakeven; it pays for the screening land-grab so equity holders get Shield's upside without (yet) needing dilutive capital.
3. **Accelerating, not decelerating.** Q1'26 +48% vs. full-year +33% guide, screening gross margin 18% → 56%, guidance raised twice — the trend is the analyst's friend.

## 10. Bear case / key risks

1. **Cash burn + dilution.** Still ~$220m adjusted-EBITDA loss and −$233m FCF in 2025; the company has repeatedly raised equity/converts (latest ~$717m, Nov 2025). If Shield's ramp slips, more dilution at lower prices is the base outcome — and the bear target reflects that.
2. **Competition arrives well-funded.** Exact Sciences (Cologuard franchise + Freenome blood test ~late 2026) has scale, a PCP salesforce and reimbursement relationships. Screening could become a price/share war just as GH is investing hardest.
3. **Clinical/guideline limits.** Shield's ~13% advanced-adenoma sensitivity means guidelines position it as a *second-line* option for those who decline colonoscopy/stool — capping share unless USPSTF and data improve. **USPSTF inclusion is not guaranteed.**
4. **Valuation leaves no room for error.** ~10–11x sales prices in continued execution; any volume miss, ASP compression, or reimbursement setback de-rates the multiple *and* the estimates simultaneously (the classic growth-stock double-hit).
5. **Reveal/MRD is a distant #2** to Natera — don't underwrite GH winning that market.

**What would change my mind (to Pass/Short):** (a) USPSTF explicitly declines blood-based screening or a negative coverage decision; (b) Shield volumes/ASP miss guidance two quarters running; (c) a credible competitor (Exact/Freenome) launching at materially better advanced-adenoma sensitivity or lower price; (d) a dilutive raise materially below the current price without a corresponding step-up in screening trajectory.

## 11. Catalysts to watch (next 12 months)

- **USPSTF re-evaluation / blood-based screening inclusion** — the single biggest value unlock.
- **Quarterly Shield volume + ASP** (guide: 230–245k tests, $186–198m '26) — proof the guideline inclusion converts to scripts.
- **Commercial-payer coverage expansion** for Shield (beyond Medicare's $1,495).
- **Exact Sciences / Freenome FDA filing & launch timing** (~late 2026) — competitive read-through.
- **Reveal (MRD) reimbursement / guideline progress** — upside optionality.
- **FCF-breakeven milestones** for the non-screening business and total-company burn trajectory.
- **Guardant360 / Guardant Infinity** new approvals and pharma (Merck) collaboration milestones.
